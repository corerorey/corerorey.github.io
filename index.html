About

An academic homepage built around active work.

This site is meant to orient visitors quickly: who I am, what I am studying, and where to start if you want to read code, notes, or a fuller profile.

I am an atmospheric science student at Lanzhou University. My current public work centers on dust-event diagnostics, environmental exposure engineering, and computational pipelines that connect atmospheric observations with health-oriented questions.

Research setting

Lanzhou, Gansu, China, with a current emphasis on Northwest China and dust-relevant environmental conditions.

Working style

Code-first, reproducible workflows that combine public data sources, diagnostics, and transparent modeling steps.

What to open first

The main research repository, the web CV, and the physics notes archive are the best starting points.

Research Focus

Dust, exposure, and interpretable environmental health analysis.

The current research thread moves from atmospheric measurements to exposure signals, then into health-facing statistical and machine-learning workflows.

The central public project explores how sand and dust storm exposure may influence respiratory infectious disease risk, with an initial focus on measles in Northwest China. The workflow is organized around two linked paths: environmental exposure engineering and downstream health modeling.

  • Dust-event diagnostics
  • Exposure engineering
  • Environmental epidemiology
  • Trajectory analysis
  • Interpretable machine learning
  • Causal workflows

Environmental inputs

CNEMC, MERRA-2, ERA5, Himawari, and HYSPLIT pipelines support event review and exposure reconstruction.

Modeling track

Current repository materials reference GAM, DLNM, Random Forest, SHAP, and DoWhy-based analytical components.

Public output

Code, workflow diagrams, representative figures, and documentation are all available through the main repository.
