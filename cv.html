Profile

A concise summary of the current public academic profile.

Cory Kong is an atmospheric science student at Lanzhou University. His current public work explores how atmospheric processes, especially sand and dust events, can be translated into structured exposure signals and studied in relation to human-health questions.

The work is computational in style, combining observational and reanalysis data, trajectory analysis, and interpretable modeling workflows. Public materials currently center on a research repository, supporting notes, and a lightweight web presence that makes active work easy to inspect.

Selected Work

Research Repository

Sand and Dust Events and Human Health

A machine-learning-assisted analytical framework for investigating how sand and dust storm exposure may influence respiratory infectious disease risk, with a current public focus on Northwest China. The repository is organized around environmental data preparation and downstream health-modeling workflows.

Referenced methods and components include CNEMC, MERRA-2, ERA5, Himawari, HYSPLIT, GAM, DLNM, Random Forest, SHAP, and DoWhy.
